Kimchi


Ingredients (Makes about 1 quart of Kimchi)

  • 1 medium Napa cabbage

  • 1/4 cup sea salt

  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger

  • 3 cloves garlic, minced

  • 1 tablespoon sugar

  • 3 tablespoons fish sauce

  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce

  • 1 tablespoon Korean red pepper flakes (Gochugaru)

  • 3 green onions, chopped

  • 1 medium carrot, julienned

Preparation

PREPARE THE CABBAGE AND MAKE THE KIMCHI PASTE

    Step 1

  1. Cut the Napa cabbage in half lengthwise, then cut each half into thirds. Sprinkle sea salt between the leaves of each cabbage wedge.

    2. Step 2

  2. Place the salted cabbage in a large bowl, cover with water, and let it soak for 2 hours. Turn the cabbage occasionally to ensure even salting.

    3. Step 3

  3. Rinse the cabbage thoroughly under cold water and drain.

    4. Step 4

  4. In a separate bowl, mix grated ginger, minced garlic, sugar, fish sauce, soy sauce, and Korean red pepper flakes to create the Kimchi paste.

    5. ASSEMBLE AND FERMENT THE KIMCHI

    Step 1

  5. Take each cabbage wedge and spread a generous amount of the Kimchi paste between the leaves, making sure to coat each leaf.

    6. Step 2

  6. Place the Kimchi-filled cabbage wedges in a clean, airtight container, pressing them down to remove air bubbles. Leave some space at the top.

    7. Step 3

  7. Top the container with chopped green onions and julienned carrots.

    8. Step 4

  8. Seal the container and let it ferment at room temperature for 1 to 5 days, depending on your desired level of fermentation. Check daily and press the Kimchi down to keep it submerged in its juices.

    9. Step 5

  9. Once fermented to your liking, refrigerate the Kimchi. It will continue to develop flavor over time.

    10. Step 6

  10. Enjoy your homemade Kimchi as a side dish, in salads, or as a topping for various dishes!
