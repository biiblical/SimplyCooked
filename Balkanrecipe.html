Bulgarian Stuffed Peppers (Vegetarian Friendly)

Palneni Chushki or Bulgarian stuffed peppers, are often made for Christmas. This Palneni Chushki recipe is made without meat, though many other Bulgarian stuffed peppers contain meat and other vegetables.


Ingredients

  • 1 kg of peppers, Remove the tops and clean out the seeds

  • 150 g risotto rice, rinced to remove excess starch

  • 1 onion

  • 1 carrot

  • 4-6 tbsp. of sunflower oil (or other vegetable oil)

  • Salt and black pepper to taste

  • 1/2 tsp chubritsa (Bulgarian spice), you can also subtitue with oregano

  • 200 g of yogurt

  • 2 eggs

  • 1-2 tbsp. of flour

  • 1 tsp. of lemon (if yogurt is not sour enough)

Preparation

Preparing the peppers:

    Step 1

  1. Preheat the oven to 180-200°.

    2. Step 2

  2. Cut the onion and carrot into small pieces and fry them in the oil on moderate heat in a large frypan until soft.

    3. Step 3

  3. Add the rice and fry until it becomes “glassy.” Usually around 3-4 mins.

    4. Step 4

  4. Add salt and pepper to taste, along with the chubritsa to the rice.

    5. Step 5

  5. Add 150 ml of warm water (rice: water 1:1) and let the rice absorb the water on low heat.

    6. Step 6

  6. Prepare peppers; Make 2 or 3 fork marks on each pepper, and sprinkle a little salt inside them.

    7. Step 7

  7. Fill the peppers loosely with the rice mixture and Place them in an oven-proof dish.

    8. Step 8

  8. Bake at 180-200° for 40 minutes.

    9. Preparing the sauce(approx 5 mins before the peppers are ready):

    Step 9

  9. Mix the eggs, the yogurt, and the flour in a saucepan.

    10. Step 10

  10. Place it on a moderate heat on the stove.

    11. Step 11

  11. Stir continuously for 3-4 minutes until it thickens. (Do not let it boil!)

When the peppers are done, remove the dish from the oven, and serve it topped with the sauce.
