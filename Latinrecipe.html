Authentic Empanadas

These delicious bites are a perfect blend of meat, vegetables, and fruit in a built-in shell and make a perfect meal that can be eaten with a fork or with your hands.


Ingredients

  • 500g ground beef

  • 1 red bell pepper

  • 1 white onion

  • 1/2 cup of olives

  • 1 garlic clove

  • 6 Eggs

  • 4 cups of all-purpose flour

  • A stick(1/2 cup) of butter

  • 2 tablespoons of tomato paste

  • 1/2 cup of raisins

  • Spices: 1 ½ teaspoons of oregano, 1 teaspoon of salt, and ½ teaspoon of pepper

  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil

  • 2 tablespoons of shortening


Preperation

1. Mixing the Dough:


  • Put the flour, salt, and pepper into a large bowl

  • stir with a wooden spoon to combine

  • Once it's well-mixed, make a dent or well in the center of the flour mix

  • Put the beaten egg, butter, shortening, and ¾ cup of water into the well

  • Mix well to bring the dough together and knead it well to make it smooth

  • Shape it into a ball

  • Cover it with plastic wrap and let it sit at room temperature for one hour

2. Preparing the filling:


  • Heat the oil in a large skillet over medium heat

  • Drop in the chopped onion and red pepper, and sauté until the onion is translucent

  • Add in the garlic and sauté for 2 more minutes

  • Add the ground beef, tomato paste, oregano, salt and pepper

  • Mix it all together and brown it

  • Transfer the contents of the skillet to a large bowl

  • Add the raisins, the chopped hard-boiled eggs, and the chopped olives

  • Stir this well to combine and set aside to cool

3. Filling the empanadas


  • Preheat the oven to 375 F

  • Use a knife to cut the dough in half and then into 20 smaller pieces

  • Roll each of these smaller pieces into a round shape

  • Drop about 2 tablespoons of the mixture into each flat chunk of empanada dough

  • Fold the dough over the filling to make a semi-circular little empanada

  • Press the edges together to close and completely lock in your empanada

  • To make those nice little divots in the edges of the empanada, use a fork

  • Beat an egg in a small bowl

  • Line up all your filled empanadas on a baking sheet

  • Brush the empanadas with the egg wash

  • Bake for 20 to 25 minutes, until the empanadas are golden brown

Serve immediately while hot, either individually plating them or offering a pile on a tray in the middle of the table.
